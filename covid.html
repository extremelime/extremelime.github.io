Does Industry Employment Affect COVID-19 Case Rates?

Jake Wigal, Dongyu Yang

The problem

There has been slow to little progress in containing COVID-19 case numbers in California, despite action at multiple levels of government meant to slow the spread of the virus by restricting the activity of non-essential economy workers and participants. There are challenges in this virus containment strategy:

  1. It can be difficult to define who fits the category of "essential workers", and
  1. it could be that workers defined as essential could be spreading the virus at high levels anyways. For example, some county economies may depend on warehouse workers in the shipping/transportation industry which are not well suited for work from home or social distancing directives.

By analyzing the most recent economic data for the CA Employment Development Department and State of California COVID-19 Health Data, we will seek to determine if there are any unique correlations between industry employment and case activity.
